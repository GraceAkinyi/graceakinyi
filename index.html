The connection

Where Africa's minerals
meet America's supply chain needs

East Africa → United States

The United States is in a race to secure critical mineral supply chains — cobalt, copper, lithium, manganese, and more. The challenge is not just demand. It is that most of these minerals come from places most supply chain professionals have never been.

I have been there. I have assessed mine sites in Kenya, DRC, and Zambia at the field level — not from a policy desk. I understand what responsible sourcing looks like on the ground, what traceability gaps actually exist, and what it takes to get suppliers to change behavior.

Now based in Reno, Nevada, I bring that firsthand knowledge of African mineral supply chains into the US conversation on critical minerals policy, supply chain security, and responsible sourcing. That is the connection I make — and it is rare.
